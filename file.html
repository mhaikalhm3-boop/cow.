Modeling Oil Palm Canopy Growth Rates via Derivatives

Understanding how fast oil palm trees grow over time is crucial for optimizing plantation density and predicting light interception. By applying calculus, we can transition from looking at total size to measuring the exact speed of growth at any given month.

The Bioscience Scenario

In Malaysian agrotech, tracking the Frond Area Index (FAI) or overall canopy radius of Elaeis guineensis (African Oil Palm) during its immature phase (Months 0 to 36) helps managers assess crop health.

Data baseline modeled after growth curves published in the Malaysian Palm Oil Board (MPOB) Palmoilis Journal and Agro-Ecological Studies of Malaysia.

The Mathematics

Growth Function, $f(x)$
f(x) = -0.00015x³ + 0.009x² + 0.05x + 0.5

Yields the estimated Canopy Radius (meters) at month $x$.

Derivative Function, $f'(x)$
f'(x) = -0.00045x² + 0.018x + 0.05

Yields the instantaneous Growth Rate (meters/month) at month $x$.
